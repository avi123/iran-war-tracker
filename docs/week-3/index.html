What Happened This Week

ENERGY WAR ESCALATION SPIRAL
Iran struck Gulf energy infrastructure overnight March 18-19: Saudi SAMREF/Yanbu + Jubail, Qatar Ras Laffan (Pearl GTL extensively damaged — LNG exports offline until June/July), Kuwait refineries. Response to Israel's South Pars strike. Brent surged to $116. Trump threatened to 'blow up' South Pars entirely. US-Israel friction: Trump rebuked strike but WSJ says he approved it in advance.
PENTAGON REQUESTS $200B SUPPLEMENTAL
Hegseth: 'that number could move' higher. Unlikely to pass Congress (needs 60 Senate votes). At Day 21, campaign cost exceeds $1B/day. DNI Gabbard testified Iran was NOT rebuilding enrichment pre-war — directly contradicts war justification. Written testimony omitted from oral statement; Sen. Warner accused her of deliberate omission.
4+ SENIOR OFFICIALS KILLED IN 48 HOURS
Larijani (SNSC Secretary/nuclear negotiator), Khatib (Intelligence Minister), G. Soleimani (Basij commander), Nasirzadeh (military chief). Hezbollah's Hassan Ali Marwan killed in Beirut. Larijani's killing closes primary diplomatic channel — he was the only experienced negotiator.
IRAN STRIKING BACK
8+ BM barrages in one night at Israel. Cluster/multiple-warhead missiles hitting central Israel: 2 killed in Ramat Gan, 4 Palestinian women killed in West Bank. Tel Aviv Savidor Central station took direct hits. Israel interceptor crisis: 'critically low' on Arrow (Semafor); NIS 2.6B ($826M) emergency defense transfer approved.
GULF STATES CHOOSE COALITION
Qatar expelled all Iranian military/security attachés (March 18). Saudi FM: trust with Iran 'completely shattered.' UAE official: attacks 'will drive Gulf into Israeli arms.' Kuwait foiled Hezbollah sabotage plot (14 Kuwaitis + 2 Lebanese arrested). Iran's Gulf strikes are strengthening, not fracturing, the coalition.
NOWRUZ TOMORROW
Mojtaba still invisible (21 days). Trump claims 'not alive.' No contact with President Pezeshkian (IranWire). Iran may not be able to pay government employees this month. Internet blackout 20 days (longest ever). Chaharshanbe Suri defiance — Iranians lit fires despite crackdown.
SOUTH PARS GAS FIELD STRUCK
first upstream energy target. IAF hit gas treatment plants (Phases 3-5-6) at Asaluyeh, Bushehr. ~1/5 of Iran's gas processing capacity damaged/offline. Iran threatens named Gulf targets in retaliation: Samref Refinery, Jubail Petrochemical, Al Hasan Gas Field.
