### Source Discipline Rules
1. **Never rely on a single source for a status change.** Require 2+ sources from different tiers.
2. **For military claims:** Cross-reference CENTCOM/IDF with independent BDA before accepting.
3. **For casualty figures:** Note source (state media vs. HRANA vs. Red Crescent). Current gap: 1,045 vs. 1,097.
4. **For market data:** Use closing prices, not intraday extremes. Market sentiment ≠ military reality.
5. **For Iranian internal dynamics:** 100hr+ blackout makes ALL ground-truth claims uncertain.
6. **For anonymous sourcing:** Flag as unconfirmed. Don't build status changes on single anonymous sources.
7. **Balance source diet:** Every cycle must include at least one FDD/hawkish source AND one skeptical source.
8. **Israeli media gap:** Hebrew outlets (Ynet, N12, Kan, Walla) publish IDF leaks and operational details 2-6 hours before English translation. When Reuters/CNN cite "Israeli media reports," they usually mean one of these. Search by outlet name to catch English-language pickups. The gap between what Israel tells international vs. domestic audiences is analytically significant for reading war aims.
10. **Gulf state media:** Reliable for specific operational data from their own countries (interception stats, airport status, infrastructure damage, national casualty figures) because incentives favor accuracy on these. NOT reliable for critical analysis of own governments, independent casualty verification, or anything embarrassing to ruling families. Use for facts, not framing.

## 2. ANALYTICAL FRAMEWORK

### Status Taxonomy (5 statuses)
| Status | Definition | Color |
|--------|-----------|-------|
| achieved | Goal met | Green |
| in progress | Underway, outcome uncertain | Amber |
| at risk | Still achievable but trajectory negative | Red |
| unachievable | Structurally impossible — no course correction can achieve this | Gray |
| tbd | Goal hasn't been tested yet — too early to assess | Gray |

### Trend Tags (applied only to "at risk" goals)
| Trend | Definition | Color | Count (Day 6) |
|-------|-----------|-------|---------------|
| failing | Current approach demonstrably not working | Red | 19 |
| expanding | Scope growing — could be enemy desperation OR loss of control | Amber | 30 |
| *(no tag)* | At risk, insufficient signal to determine direction | — | 7 |

**Key principle:** Failing and expanding are different things. A war can be militarily succeeding, geographically expanding, and politically failing simultaneously. The trend taxonomy prevents collapsing these distinct dynamics.

**No-tag goals** (7): Data genuinely doesn't point in a direction. Mostly Iranian internal dynamics behind 100hr blackout. Absence of a tag IS the signal.

**Difference between TBD status and no-tag trend:** TBD = goal hasn't been tested yet (e.g., mines not laid). No-tag = goal has been tested but result is unreadable (e.g., IRGC defections — zero at Day 6 is expected, not diagnostic).

### Current Distribution (Day 9 Morning)

- 4 unachievable

### Status Change Rules
- **To "achieved":** 2+ independent source confirmation.
- **To "at risk":** Evidence of negative trajectory, not just absence of progress.
- **To "unachievable":** STRICT test: "Could ANY realistic course correction make this possible?" If yes → at risk.
- **Reversals allowed:** Reverse and note why.

### Unachievable Standard (4 goals only)
1. **nuke-knowledge** — can't bomb physics
2. **cas-zero** — 6 KIA, irreversible
3. **dom-midterm** — war exists, it's an issue (but valence TBD)
4. **dom-predict** — insider trades happened, irreversible

**Lesson learned:** "Nobody is doing it" ≠ "nobody CAN do it." Absence of progress = at risk. Structural impossibility = unachievable.

### Bias Checks — Apply Every Cycle
1. **Iraq 2003 Prior:** Am I defaulting to "military success → political failure" without evidence?
2. **Source Diet:** Read at least one hawkish AND one skeptical source this cycle?
3. **Asymmetric Skepticism:** Equal standards for US/Israeli and Iranian claims?
4. **Timeframe:** Kosovo took 78 days. Am I judging Day 6 by Week 8 standards?
5. **Narrative Gravity:** Am I being pulled toward "everything failing" or "everything succeeding" when data is mixed?
6. **Expanding ≠ Failing:** Am I conflating scope growth with failure? Tag as expanding and present both readings.
7. **Unachievable Check:** Strict structural test before marking anything unachievable.

### Succeeding (Green)
Military metrics, coalition growth, enemy capability decline. Emphasizes FDD data, CENTCOM metrics, Gulf defense rates, Kosovo prior. Argues expansion = enemy desperation.

Scope growing on every axis. Analytically ambiguous — same facts support opposite readings. Poses the key question: does expansion decelerate (bullish) or accelerate (bearish)?

### Failing (Red)
ONLY the "failing" trend goals. Specific, identifiable, correctable breakdowns. Should note which failures have course corrections in progress.

## 9a. SPOT-CHECK PROTOCOL (Post-Research, Pre-Edit)

**Purpose:** Catch factual errors introduced by subagents or fast research before they reach the tracker. The Ron Arad/Nabi Chit error (Day 8: subagent placed a Lebanon raid inside Iran) demonstrated that subagents can confidently assert wrong locations, countries, or attributions.

### When to Run
After all research agents complete, BEFORE editing `iran-war-goals.jsx`. Takes ~5 minutes.

### Check Categories

**1. Geography Spot-Check (highest error rate)**
For every claim involving a LOCATION (city, country, facility, base):
- Does the stated country match the source?
- Is the city in that country? (e.g., Nabi Chit is in Lebanon, not Iran)
- Cross-reference: if a source says "Baalbek district," confirm which country that's in

**2. Attribution Spot-Check**
For every claim involving WHO DID WHAT:
- Which military/group is claimed to have acted?
- Does the source actually say that actor, or a different one?
- Check: IDF vs CENTCOM vs coalition — who did the strike?

**3. Number Spot-Check**
Pick 3 key numbers from the research (casualties, intercepted missiles, oil prices):
- Re-check against the cited source
- Verify units (barrels vs dollars, daily vs cumulative, dead vs wounded)
- Check if number is from today or a previous day being re-cited

**4. Temporal Spot-Check**
For claims marked as "new" or "Day X":
- Is this actually new, or a re-report of a previous day's event?
- Check timestamps in sources vs. last build timestamp

**5. Negation/Reversal Spot-Check**
For any claim where something DIDN'T happen or was DENIED:
- Does the source say "denied" or "confirmed"?
- Is the subagent reporting a denial as a confirmation or vice versa?

### How to Run
1. Pick the 5 most consequential claims from the research batch
2. For each, re-read the ACTUAL source URL (not the subagent's summary)
3. Verify: location, actor, number, timeframe, positive/negative
4. If ANY fail: re-check ALL claims from that subagent

## 9b. HIGHLIGHTS UPDATE PROTOCOL

**Purpose:** Maintain the `HIGHLIGHTS` constant in `iran-war-goals.jsx`. This carries analytical context between update cycles and gives readers a "what just happened / what to watch" summary.

### When to Run
After goal edits, BEFORE build. Takes ~3 minutes.

### Steps

**1. Resolve Previous Watch Items**
Read the current `HIGHLIGHTS.watchNext` array. For each item:
- Did it happen? → **Remove from `watchNext`**, add to `watchResolved` with `status: "confirmed"` and `resolved: "what happened"`
- Was it wrong? → **Remove from `watchNext`**, add to `watchResolved` with `status: "wrong"` and explanation
- Partially? → **Keep in `watchNext`** (update text if needed), ALSO add to `watchResolved` with `status: "partial"` and explanation
- Still developing? → **Keep in `watchNext`** (update text if needed), ALSO add to `watchResolved` with `status: "ongoing"` and latest update

**RULE: Items only leave `watchNext` when status is "confirmed" or "wrong". Partial/ongoing items stay in BOTH lists.**

- Keep max 8 resolved items (drop oldest confirmed/wrong first)

**2. Write Key Developments (3-6 items)**
Select from this cycle's findings. What makes the cut:
- Status changes (goal moved between states)
- New categories of action (first X of the war)
- Contradictions (A said X, then Y happened)
- Threshold crossings (approaching/crossing $100 oil, interceptor depletion)
- Intelligence reveals (classified reports, leaked assessments)
- NOT: routine metric updates, incremental numbers

Each item requires:
- `text`: What happened (factual, concise)
- `why`: One sentence on strategic significance — why a reader should care. Not editorializing; explain the *implication* or *mechanism*. Good: "Gap between stated war aims and intel assessment is the central tension — if regime change isn't achievable, what's the exit?" Bad: "This is very concerning."
- `category`: military | political | economic | regime | humanitarian
- `goalIds`: array of related goal IDs
- `sources`: array of `{t:"Label",u:"url"}` — same format as goal sources. At least one per item.

**3. Write Watch Items (3-6 items)**
Timeframe categories:
- `imminent` (6-12h): known upcoming events, expected military actions
- `24-48h`: approaching thresholds, scheduled meetings, expected inflections
- `this week`: developing situations, trend reversals, diplomatic windows
- `open question`: testable analytical questions without time bound

Good watch items are SPECIFIC and RESOLVABLE. Bad: "watch oil prices." Good: "Oil approaching $100/bbl — WTI at $90.90, Goldman threshold is 5 weeks of Hormuz disruption."

Each item requires:
- `text`: What to watch (specific, testable)
- `why`: One sentence on why this matters — what changes if it resolves one way vs another
- `category`, `timeframe`, `sources`: same format as key developments

**4. Update `updatedAt` timestamp**
Use `date -u +"%Y-%m-%dT%H:%MZ"` or the build.sh output timestamp. NEVER approximate or guess.

### Accountability Rule
Every watch item must eventually be resolved. If an item sits as "ongoing" for 3+ cycles, either:
- Sharpen it (make it more specific/testable)
- Resolve it (conclude the signal wasn't diagnostic)
- Elevate it to a new goal/subgoal if it's become significant enough
